Program Spotlight — Four Core Activities

Santa Monica College is enhancing four interlocking initiatives — professional development interdepartmental workshops/trainings, conference participation, a mentorship program pairing 10 employees with 10 mentors, and five active Employee Resource Groups (ERGs). All four follow a consistent annual cycle of planning, implementation, feedback, and refinement, and are administered by SMC’s Human Resources team in collaboration with CSEA, the New Faculty Committee, and ERG leadership.

ACTIVITY 1Professional Development

Expand leadership training, diversity education, and skills-building workshops for employees and departments to provide tools for their employment experiences and career paths.

Activities

  • Open a District-wide call for proposals; departments submit development opportunities across several areas.
  • Internal HR grants committee reviews and approves proposals based on cost, outcomes, and grant objectives.
  • Offer three to four sessions annually — one District-wide serving 200 employees, the remainder serving up to six departments.
  • Fund consultants (up to $5,000 each) and catering per approved session.
  • Collect participation, demographic, and feedback data with the Office of Institutional Research.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    Three to four workshops conducted per year.
    AssessmentWorkshop tracking, attendance and demographic data.
  2. 02
    Knowledge gain and confidence applying inclusive practices.
    AssessmentPre-and-post training surveys.
  3. 03
    Department proposals submitted and funded.
    AssessmentProposal tracking; follow-up implementation reports or action plans.
ACTIVITY 2Conference Participation

Expand opportunities for staff to attend leadership training, diverse educational opportunities, and skills-based workshops through selected conferences that increase EEO competencies.

Activities

  • HR reviews and selects conferences that meet grant objectives, with suggestions from constituency groups.
  • Create a transparent, inclusive nomination process prioritizing staff with potential to apply learnings District-wide.
  • Support two to three participants at two to three conferences each year — registration, transportation, lodging, and meal per diems.
  • Participants share learnings through peer and departmental presentations after each conference.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    Two to three staff attend two to three conferences annually.
    AssessmentAttendance tracking; diversity of participants by role and department.
  2. 02
    Internal presentations or knowledge-sharing sessions post-conference.
    AssessmentSession counts; follow-up reports or presentations.
  3. 03
    Application of learning in daily practice.
    AssessmentQualitative feedback from supervisors and departments.
ACTIVITY 3Mentorship Programs

Enhance retention strategies and establish mentorship initiatives that pair employees with mentors and leaders to offer structured guidance, professional networking, and leadership development.

Activities

  • Partner with the New Faculty Committee and CSEA to identify new hires and seasoned employees.
  • Match 10 mentor-mentee pairs (20 participants) based on role, goals, and areas of growth — a 50% increase over the seven pairs in the 2023–25 grant.
  • Provide stipends of $400 per participant plus classified overtime and fringe benefits.
  • Hold four structured mentor-mentee meetings per year with orientation and resources.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    10 mentor-mentee pairs formed annually.
    AssessmentPairing records based on constituency-group recommendations.
  2. 02
    Four meetings held per pair per year.
    AssessmentMeeting logs; participant satisfaction surveys.
  3. 03
    Retention of mentees and mentors over the two-year period.
    AssessmentRetention tracked in the biannual District-wide Diversity Report.
ACTIVITY 4Employee Resource Groups

Enhance employee retention, engagement, and a stronger sense of belonging through five active Employee Resource Groups (ERGs), contributing to a more inclusive workplace culture.

Activities

  • Support the five ERGs identified and activated under the 2023–25 EEO IBP grant.
  • ERGs submit funding plans for workshops, retreats, and cultural events that build belonging and retention.
  • Allocate $3,000 per ERG per year — $2,000 or more for consultant fees and no more than $1,000 for catering.
  • Each ERG meets one to two times per year and shares outcomes with the broader staff.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    One to two meetings or events per ERG annually.
    AssessmentFunding plans; event tracking.
  2. 02
    Membership engagement and participation growth.
    AssessmentEngagement rates and participation data.
  3. 03
    Strengthened sense of belonging, community impact, and inclusivity.
    AssessmentEvent feedback surveys.
How the activities connect
Application Narrative · Section 1.3

Together, these four core activities form an equity-centered model supporting inclusive retention, workforce diversity, and professional development at Santa Monica College. SMC serves 27,000 students throughout greater Los Angeles County, and the District strives for its faculty and staff to mirror that culturally rich student population. Each activity follows a structured approach — needs-based design, equitable access, targeted support, and ongoing evaluation — with SMC’s HR team collaborating District-wide with the New Faculty Committee, CSEA, ERGs, and the Office of Institutional Research to measure impact and effectiveness.

Workplan Activities
By core activity · from Section 2.2
ACTIVITY 1 · Workshops
  • Open a District-wide call for proposals. Departments submit proposals supporting equitable retention strategies.
  • Internal HR grants committee review. Proposals selected on reasonable cost, outcomes, and grant objectives — funds allocated first-come-first-serve.
  • Sponsor workshops and trainings. Equitable recruitment, screening, and retention practices, with consultants and catering funded.
  • Collaborate with CSEA, the New Faculty Association, and ERGs. Recruit participants and identify workshop topics.
  • Track participation and collect feedback. Employee experiences, sense of belonging, and skills-building.
ACTIVITY 2 · Conferences
  • Fund and coordinate conference attendance. Targeted conferences on diversity, equity, leadership, and organizational development.
  • Transparent, inclusive nomination process. Built with constituency groups, prioritizing staff with District-wide impact.
  • Integrate insights. Participants deliver follow-up reports or presentations to fold conference learnings into training and retention strategies.
  • Cover full participation costs. Registration, transportation, lodging, meal per diems, and other relevant expenses.
ACTIVITY 3 · Mentorship
  • Identify participants with the New Faculty Committee and CSEA. New hires and seasoned employees who benefit from structured mentorship.
  • Match mentees with trained mentors. Based on role, goals, and areas of growth.
  • Structured mentor-mentee meetings. Professional development, leadership training, and knowledge transfer — four times per year.
  • Fund stipends, overtime, and catering. $400 stipends per participant, classified overtime, fringe benefits, and session catering.
ACTIVITY 4 · ERGs
  • Support ERG-led events. Training, workshops, retreats, and cultural events that build community and belonging.
  • Review ERG funding plans. Each of the five ERGs submits a proposal on how funds will promote retention and internal support systems.
  • Fund consultants and coordination. Speakers, facilitators, and event catering — $3,000 per ERG per year.
  • Monitor outcomes. Feedback from participants measuring belonging, community impact, and inclusivity.
Institutional Resources Used
  • Human Resources Department. Manages grant administration and day-to-day coordination; skilled in program coordination, training facilitation, and data tracking from the 2023–2025 EEO IBP grant.
  • Internal HR grants committee. Two HR Managers, two departmental assistants, an HR Technician/Analyst, and the Professional Development Coordinator review and accept proposals.
  • CSEA & New Faculty Committee. Lead outreach, recruitment, and mentor-mentee pairing for the Mentorship Programs.
  • Office of Institutional Research. Evaluation design, advanced data analysis, benchmarking, and strategic insights.
  • Fiscal Services & Grants Office. Designated grant accountant oversees fund disbursement, stipends, and financial compliance; IT and Facilities provide logistical and technological support.
Program Model & Methodology

Each activity follows a structured approach grounded in best practices for organizational development and employee engagement (Section 1.3):

  • Needs-Based Design. HR works with campus groups to identify priorities for development, mentorship, and community building
  • Equitable Access. Open proposal and selection processes promote fair access, prioritizing equity, impact, and cost-effectiveness
  • Targeted Support. Programs address key retention and advancement needs, with funding for consultants, materials, and implementation
  • Ongoing Evaluation. Participation, outcomes, and feedback drive continuous improvement in engagement, leadership diversity, and retention
